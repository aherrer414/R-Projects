Description of Data

Airbnb, Inc is an online marketplace for arranging or offering lodging mostly for homestays or tourism thus acting as a broker in receiving commissions between a host and guest.

Airbnb is part of the sharing economy in which individuals are able to make money off their property and many variables affect a successful host in their longevity in using the app as well as the financial gains they have.

The dataset describes the listing of activity and metric of Airbnb activity in New York City, New York for the year 2019.

The data sets contains information about the hosts, geographic availability, price of lodging, information about the reviews, and necessary metrics to make predictions and conclusions.

The data will be able to help learn about hosts in different areas of New York such as which hosts are busiest or which characteristics have the potential to make the most money, this can help explore Airbnb trends in New York City.

###Question 4 How does the average price vary between the type of rooms avaialbe in Manhattan?

airbnb %>%
  filter(neighbourhood_group == 'Manhattan')%>%
  group_by(room_type) %>%
  summarise(average_price_room = mean(price, na.rm = TRUE))%>%
ggplot(aes(x = room_type, y = average_price_room)) +
  geom_bar(stat="identity", fill = 'dark green', color = 'black')

#Explanation A bar graph seemed most appropriate becuase we wanted to find the different price averages of the type of rooms specfically in Manhattan. A bar graph works because it help differentiate between an entire home or apartment, private room, or shared room. This can help people determine the difference bewteen the groups and how much they would save between the different groups of type of room. Manhattan is where there is a lot of tourism and well as business so it would be helpful to know the average price there. The type of rooms is helpful since it also limits the amount for groups and gives an idea of what is best in price for big or small groups or people travling alonge.
